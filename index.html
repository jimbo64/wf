Still want to play football, but dont have the same stamina and pace that you once had? Then walking football is ideal for you. Even if you still play, walking football is still a challenge and might even help improve your touch, it's also a great way of getting fit.

In general, it's aimed at the 50+ age group (there is no upper age limit), but we encourage those at 45+ who fancy playing again to come along. We meet at the Arbroath high school sport centre every Wednesday. The session costs Â£3.00 and starts at 19.15 for an hour, but we would appreciate it if you were there for 19.00, this helps us sort out the admin and gives us the full hour for playing. We are a non affiliated, non profit club, any money left over from the hire costs will be used for the benefit of the club.

Latest

Arbroath walking football club had an exhibition match against Carnoustie Panmure walking football club at Gayfield park yesterday.
The game was played during the half time interval of Arbroath's Scottish cup tie against Stirling university, which saw Arbroath progress to the next round with a handsome 3-1 victory
A great day was had by both teams, and we would like to thank all who were involved in the organisation (particulary Brian Cargill and Sye Webster).
We hope that we have shown that it's not about a bunch of old men with zimmer frames. We also hope that we might have picked up a few more recruits for the walking football in both Arbroath and Carnoustie.
